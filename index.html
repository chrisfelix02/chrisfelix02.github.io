About

I’m Christopher Felix, a computer engineer with a strong focus on hardware design and Very Large Scale Integration (VLSI). During my undergraduate career, I majored in Computer Engineering at the University of Michigan and I am currently completing my graduate career in VLSI and Computing Systems at Florida International University.

My passion lies in designing and understanding systems at the silicon level: from RTL and microarchitecture to integrated circuits and hardware verification. Through VLSI and Computer Architecture, I aim to help build technology that is smarter, faster, and more compact, while remaining cost-efficient and scalable. I enjoy working at the intersection of theory and practice, whether that means debugging waveforms, optimizing datapaths, or translating architectural ideas into real hardware. Outside of engineering, I’m a curious problem-solver who enjoys basketball, soccer, reading, cooking, traveling, gaming, and music. I regularly (almost everyday) consume new music, so I'm always open to discuss your fav whether it be Bad Bunny, Selena Quintanilla, Shakira, Nas, or even The Weeknd, just to name a few.🎤

I believe good engineers build great systems, and well-rounded engineers build systems that last. ⚙️
